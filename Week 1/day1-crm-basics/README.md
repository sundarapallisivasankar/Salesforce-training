# Day 1 – CRM Basics | Salesforce Summer Program

## What is CRM?

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management. It is used by companies to manage customer data, communication, sales, and business activities in one place.

CRM helps businesses improve customer relationships and increase productivity.

---

## Why Salesforce is Used

Salesforce is a popular CRM platform used by many companies because it helps to:

* Store customer information securely
* Track sales and opportunities
* Automate business processes
* Improve customer communication
* Generate reports and insights

Salesforce is cloud-based and easy to customize.

---

# Important Salesforce Concepts

## Account

Represents a company or organization.
Examples: Infosys, Amazon, TCS

## Contact

Represents a person related to an Account.
Examples: Customer, Employee, Student

## Opportunity

Represents a potential business deal or sale.

---

# Salesforce Business Flow

Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer

* Lead: Person interested in a service or product
* Contact: Qualified lead
* Opportunity: Possible business deal
* Customer: Completed deal

---

# Real-Time Example (College Admission)

* Account  - College
* Contact  - Student
* Lead     - Student interested in admission
* opportunity - Admission process
